Portuguese Custard Tarts

A Donna Hay recipe I have simplified to be more efficient, with only a saucepan and tray to clean. Essentially you make an egg vanilla custard and bake it in puff pastry.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry
  • 4 tablespoons of Castor Sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of Corn Flour
  • 2 cups of Milk
  • 1 egg yolk from a free range egg
  • 1 ½ teaspoons of vanilla essence

Method:

  1. Thaw a sheet of frozen pastry - that's right, it makes little difference and is a whole lot easier.
  2. Preheat oven to 190°C.
  3. Add the castor sugar and cornflour to the saucepan.
  4. Add a small amount of the milk and dissolve the ingredients to a paste.
  5. Add the rest of the milk
  6. Add 1 ½ teaspoons of vanilla essence.
  7. Beat in the egg yoke.
  8. Heat the custard, stirring until it thickens.
  9. Cut the pastry into 6 circles with a broad tea cup.
  10. Push them into a muffin tray and bake them empty a bit while the custard cools
  11. Spoon the custard into the pastry shells and bake for around 20 minutes - until they brown like burnt creme brule.
