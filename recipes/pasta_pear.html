Italian style Pear Pasta

This is an idea I stole from an italian joint in Melboune. Pear pasta. To me it was interesting and unusual.
I liked it so much I gradually reverse engineered it so I could enjoy it at home and make it for others.
I'm just about there, I think. With a glass of reisling on a summer's night - it's perfect. Enjoy!

Ingredients:

  • slice of butter for each person
  • Half a small Onion for each person
  • 1 pear for each person
  • Walnuts (a handfull crushed for each person)
  • 1 frac12; teaspoons Raw Sugar for each person
  • Parmesan cheese sliced into small chips or shavings
  • Olive Oil
  • Ground Rock salt and Pepper corns
  • Fresh Parsley

Method:

  1. Put water on to boil.
  2. Add butter to the pan (or Olive Oil but butter is the way if you want the best taste)
  3. Dice the onion and fry in Butter until translucent.
  4. Add raw sugar to the pan and disolve into the sauce
  5. Cook pasta
  6. Slice the Pear about 3mm thick and cook it in the pan with a little splash of water for heat conduction.
  7. Make the table respectable.
  8. Slice Parmesan cheese
  9. Drain pasta and toss it through the sauce.
  10. Make the table respectable.
  11. Serve with slices of parmeson on top and then grate over pepper and salt
  12. Top with fresh parsley.
